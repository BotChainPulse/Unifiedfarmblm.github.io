Building Your First Chicken Coop on a Budget

A good chicken coop does not need to cost a fortune. At Unified Farm BLM, our first coop was built from scrap wood, old iron sheets, and mesh wire we found around the farm. It housed 20 hens comfortably and cost less than UGX 80,000. That same coop still stands today — proof that a well-built budget coop can last for years.

This guide walks you through building a functional, predator-proof chicken coop using materials you can find locally in Uganda. The design houses 15–20 hens, provides proper ventilation, and keeps your flock safe from dogs, snakes, and wild cats.

What You Will Need

Total Budget: UGX 80,000 – 100,000. Use recycled materials to bring the cost down to UGX 50,000.
Material Quantity Estimated Cost (UGX) Where to Find
Timber poles (eucalyptus) 12 pieces (3m each) 25,000 Local timber yard or plantation
Corrugated iron sheets 4–5 sheets 30,000 Recycled from old buildings
Chicken wire mesh 5 meters 15,000 Agricultural supply shop
Nails (4-inch and 2-inch) 1 kg 8,000 Hardware store
Hinges and latch 1 set 5,000 Hardware store
Grass or papyrus for thatching (optional) Bundle 5,000 Local market or wetland

Step-by-Step Construction

1 Choose the Right Location

Select a spot that is:

  • Well-drained: Avoid low-lying areas where water collects during the rainy season.
  • Shaded: Partial shade from a tree helps keep the coop cool in Uganda's hot afternoons.
  • Elevated: Build on slightly raised ground to prevent flooding.
  • Accessible: You will visit the coop twice daily, so place it near your house.
Predator Warning: Keep the coop at least 2 meters away from fences and walls. Predators use these as launching points to climb into the coop.

2 Build the Frame

Cut four timber poles to 2 meters for the vertical posts. Dig holes 30cm deep at each corner and set the posts firmly in the ground. Connect the tops with horizontal poles to create a rectangular frame measuring approximately 2m x 1.5m.

Pro Tip: Treat the bottom 30cm of each post with used engine oil or old paint. This prevents termites from eating the wood underground.

3 Add the Walls

Attach chicken wire mesh to three sides of the frame using nails or U-nails. The fourth side will be your door. For the lower 60cm of the walls, use solid boards or thick papyrus mats to block wind and provide privacy for the nesting area.

4 Build the Roof

Create a sloped roof using iron sheets or thatched grass. The slope should angle toward the back so rainwater drains away from the entrance. A sloped roof also helps reflect heat — keeping the interior cooler during hot days.

If using iron sheets, place a layer of papyrus or dry grass underneath. This provides insulation and reduces the noise of rain hitting the metal.

5 Create the Nesting Boxes

Build three nesting boxes along one wall, each measuring 30cm x 30cm x 40cm. Place them 30cm above the floor and add a small perch in front so hens can easily enter. Line each box with dry grass or soft cloth.

Rule of Thumb: Provide one nesting box for every 4–5 hens. For a flock of 20, four boxes are ideal.

6 Add Perches (Roosting Bars)

Install two horizontal perches at different heights (40cm and 60cm from the floor). Use smooth, round poles about 5cm in diameter. Space them 30cm apart horizontally. Chickens prefer to sleep on perches — it keeps them off the damp floor and reduces parasite exposure.

7 Build the Door and Secure the Coop

Construct a simple door using timber frame and wire mesh. Attach it with hinges and add a secure latch. Predators in Uganda — especially dogs and genets — are clever. Use a latch that requires a sliding motion, not just a hook.

8 Prepare the Floor

Spread a 10cm layer of dry grass, wood shavings, or chopped banana leaves on the floor. This bedding absorbs moisture and makes cleaning easier. Replace the bedding every 2–3 weeks and compost the old material for your garden.

Coop Maintenance Schedule

Task Frequency Time Required
Replace bedding Every 2–3 weeks 15 minutes
Clean nesting boxes Weekly 10 minutes
Check for holes/gaps Monthly 10 minutes
Repair wire mesh As needed 20 minutes
Deep clean (disinfect) Every 3 months 1 hour

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Making the coop too small: Each hen needs at least 0.3 square meters of floor space. A 2m x 1.5m coop (3 sq meters) houses 10 hens comfortably. Crowding causes stress, feather-pecking, and disease.
2. Poor ventilation: A completely enclosed coop traps heat and ammonia. Always leave gaps near the roof for air circulation, covered with wire mesh to keep predators out.
3. Neglecting the door latch: We lost 8 chickens in one night because a loosely hooked door swung open. Always double-check your latch at night.

Upgrading Your Coop Over Time

As your flock grows, upgrade the coop incrementally:

  • Month 1–3: Basic coop as described above.
  • Month 4–6: Add a covered run area where chickens can move freely during the day.
  • Month 7–12: Install a rainwater collection system for automatic water supply.
  • Year 2: Build a second coop to separate broilers from layers.
