Best Chicken Breeds for Uganda: Local vs Imported Guide

Choosing the right chicken breed is the most important decision you will make as a poultry farmer. The wrong breed for your goals will waste money, time, and effort. At Unified Farm BLM, we have raised five different breeds over three years — from local village chickens to imported commercial hybrids. This guide compares every major breed available in Uganda so you can choose the perfect match for your farm.

Detailed Breed Profiles

Best for Beginners

Kuroiler (Keggfarms, India)

The Kuroiler is the most popular dual-purpose breed in Uganda for good reason. It grows fast, produces reasonable eggs, and thrives on low-input management. Kuroilers can scavenge for 60–70% of their diet, making them ideal for farmers with limited feed budgets.

  • Growth: Males reach 3.5–4 kg in 5 months; females reach 2.5–3 kg
  • Eggs: 150–200 brown eggs per year
  • Feed efficiency: Can survive on kitchen scraps and scavenging
  • Hardiness: Resistant to most common diseases; tolerates heat well
  • Price: UGX 3,000–4,500 per day-old chick
Our experience: Kuroilers were our first breed at Unified Farm BLM. We started with 20 birds and lost only 2 in the first year. They are forgiving, hardy, and profitable for beginners.
Best for Egg Production

ISA Brown (France)

The ISA Brown is the world champion of egg production. If your goal is maximum eggs with commercial efficiency, this is your breed. However, ISAs require high-quality feed, proper housing, and strict vaccination schedules.

  • Growth: Lightweight birds (1.8–2.2 kg), not ideal for meat
  • Eggs: 300–320 large brown eggs per year
  • Feed efficiency: Requires 110–120g of layer feed daily
  • Hardiness: Moderate; susceptible to heat stress and disease
  • Price: UGX 4,500–6,000 per day-old chick
Important: ISA Browns are NOT suitable for free-range or scavenging systems. They need commercial feed, clean water, and proper housing to reach their egg potential. Do not buy ISAs if you cannot provide these conditions.
Best for Meat

Cobb 500 Broiler (USA)

The Cobb 500 is the industry standard for commercial broiler production. These birds are bred for one purpose: converting feed into meat as fast as possible. A Cobb 500 reaches 2 kg in just 35–42 days.

  • Growth: 2.0–2.5 kg in 6–7 weeks
  • Eggs: Poor layer; not kept for eggs
  • Feed efficiency: 1.6–1.8 kg feed per 1 kg of weight gain (excellent)
  • Hardiness: Low; needs temperature-controlled housing and strict biosecurity
  • Price: UGX 3,500–4,500 per day-old chick
Best for Free-Range

Local Village Chicken (Indigenous)

The indigenous Ugandan chicken is the ultimate survivor. These birds scavenge, resist disease, and require almost zero inputs. Their meat commands premium prices in urban markets because consumers prefer the taste and texture.

  • Growth: Slow — 6–9 months to reach 1.5–2 kg
  • Eggs: Only 40–60 small eggs per year
  • Feed efficiency: Minimal; mostly scavenging
  • Hardiness: Extremely high; almost disease-proof
  • Price: UGX 1,000–2,500 per chick; mature birds sell for UGX 25,000–40,000
Premium market: Local chickens sell for 2–3x the price of broilers in Kampala restaurants. A 2 kg local bird fetches UGX 35,000–50,000 vs UGX 15,000–20,000 for a broiler.

Which Breed Should You Choose?

Your Situation Recommended Breed Why
Total beginner, limited budget Kuroiler or Local Village Hardy, low input, forgiving of mistakes
Goal: maximum eggs ISA Brown 300+ eggs/year with proper care
Goal: fast meat production Cobb 500 or Ross 308 2 kg in 6–7 weeks
Free-range, low-input system Kuroiler, Sasso, or Local Can scavenge and tolerate harsh conditions
Mixed meat + eggs Rainbow Rooster or Kuroiler Good balance of both products
Premium meat market Local Village or Sasso Consumers pay premium for taste and quality

Where to Buy Quality Chicks in Uganda

  • Ugachick Poultry Breeders: Leading supplier of broilers, layers, and dual-purpose breeds. Multiple locations including Kampala and Entebbe.
  • Biyinzika Poultry International: Wide variety of breeds including Kuroilers, ISAs, and broilers. Good reputation for healthy chicks.
  • Vetline Poultry: Specialist in vaccinated day-old chicks and poultry equipment.
  • Local hatcheries: Many district-level hatcheries supply Kuroilers and local breeds at lower prices. Ask at your local agricultural office.
Buying Tip: Always inspect chicks before purchase. Healthy day-old chicks should be active, dry (not wet or pasted), and have bright eyes. Reject any batch with more than 2% dead or weak chicks.
